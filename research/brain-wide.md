Through the International Brain Laboratory -- a collaboration of 22 labs building a standardized experimental platform -- we helped construct the first brain-wide map of decision-making, spanning 279 regions across hundreds of sessions. The map reveals that choice signals are distributed across the brain rather than localized in a few canonical areas. Our specific contribution is neuromodulation: recording serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine, and acetylcholine during the same standardized task to understand how these systems interact across the full decision circuit.
