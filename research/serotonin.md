How does serotonin shape the brain's capacity to decide? We track serotonin neurons in real time during behavior, using optogenetic tools and electrophysiology developed in the lab over the past decade. A key discovery is that serotonin modulates neural activity in dimensions orthogonal to those encoding decisions -- it shifts brain state without disrupting the computations already underway. To understand how serotonin compares to other modulators, we run a large-scale fiber photometry survey recording six neuromodulatory systems head-to-head during the same behavioral task.
