Mice make faces -- and those faces carry information about cognition, not just emotion. Using machine vision and simultaneous neural recordings, we have shown that mouse facial expressions predict variables like surprise and confidence, suggesting that expressions evolved partly as readouts of internal computation rather than purely as social signals. We are now extending this approach to ask whether the same principle holds in humans: whether the face is a window not just into feeling, but into thinking.
