Psychedelics produce profound alterations in conscious experience through mechanisms we are only beginning to understand at the neural level. Using Neuropixels recordings in mice given LSD, we have found that the drug restructures neural variability in a timescale-dependent way -- increasing moment-to-moment fluctuations while decreasing slower patterns of coordination, a signature that may relate to the subjective dissolution of stable percepts. We connect this rigorous neurophysiology with serious engagement with phenomenology and consciousness science, working to bridge the gap between what neurons do and what subjects experience.
